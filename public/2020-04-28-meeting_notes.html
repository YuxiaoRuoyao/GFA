Eigenvector correction

\[ \hat{Z}U = LF^\top U + \Theta U + E U \]

Fixed factor correction

This strategy is based on ideas from Matthew and Jason here. Briefly, let \(\lambda_{min}\) be the smallest eigenvalue of \(R\) and let \(W = R - \lambda_{min}I\). Let \(v_1, \dots, v_M\) be eigenvectors of \(W\) and \(a_1, \dots, a_M\) eigenvalues of \(W\). Then we can decompose \(E\) as

\[ E = \sum_{k=1}^n v_k t_k^T + E^{\prime} \] where the elements of \(E^{\prime}\) are iid \(E^{\prime}_{ij} \sim N(0, \lambda_{min})\) ant \(t_k\) are \(J\times 1\) vectors with iid elements \(t_ki \sim N(0, a_k)\). This means that we can fit the model by including \(M\) fixed factors \(v_1, \dots, v_M\) and specifying the prior for the associated loadings.
